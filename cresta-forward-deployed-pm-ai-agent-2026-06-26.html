01The bet

Enterprise agentic AI does not fail because the models are weak. It fails at deployment.

Integration, trust, and use-case scoping are where pilots stall. Only 27% of CX teams that piloted agentic AI reached production (2026 CX AI agent statistics).

My bet for this role: I would own the deployment lifecycle per account so agents reach production fast and prove ROI, then turn every deployment into a reusable playbook and platform feedback so the next one is faster. Repeatability is the moat. One-off custom work is the trap.

02The problem

Cresta is at roughly $100M ARR and growing faster than ever (Cresta, 2026). At that stage the constraint is not the product. Cresta shipped four to five major products in 2025 alone. The constraint is deployment velocity: turning pilots into live production agents, and live agents into expansion revenue. The forward deployed PM closes that gap, account by account.

64% → 27% ran an agentic AI pilot, but only this many put a channel into full production.
25% of business leaders trust AI agents in autonomous customer interactions.
8 to 10 systems a Fortune 500 agent touches, most without real-time APIs.

Reading the problem in plain terms

  • Who hurts. The customer's CX leader who bought an outcome and is waiting on it, and Cresta, which carries the cost of a stalled deployment. The end customer who reaches an agent that cannot resolve their issue hurts too.
  • What the failure looks like. Pilots stall in the gap between a demo and a production system. In 2026, 64% of enterprise CX teams ran a pilot but only 27% put at least one channel into full production (2026 CX AI agent statistics).
  • Root cause. Cresta's CEO Ping Wu put it directly: Fortune 500 agents touch 8 to 10 systems that lack real-time APIs. The hard part is integration, PII handling, data residency, call transfers, latency across regions, and multi-language support, not the model (Ping Wu, Sequoia podcast).
  • The trust wall. Only 25% of business leaders trust AI agents in autonomous interactions (Cresta, Sept 2025). Hallucination complaints are only 0.34% of AI-handled tickets, yet 71% of CX leaders rank them a top-three governance risk because each public failure is costly (2026 CX AI agent statistics). A skeptical VP will not sign off on go-live without evidence the agent is safe.
  • What it costs. A human-handled contact runs $5 to $12. An enterprise running 12 million contacts a year and targeting 20% containment is looking at $12M to $28.8M in annual cost reduction. A deployment that slips 60 days loses roughly $2M to $5M in annualized ROI (estimate, derived from the brief's contact economics).
This is a race

Sierra raised $950M at a $15.8B valuation (TechCrunch, May 2026) and is at roughly $200M ARR (Sacra, 2026). Decagon raised $250M at a $4.5B valuation (Sacra, 2026). Salesforce Agentforce attacks from the CRM side. At $100M ARR, Cresta does not defend its position with more demos. It defends it by getting agents into production faster than anyone else and proving ROI that renews and expands. That is the forward deployed PM's job.

03Who it is for

Job to be done means the outcome someone hires the product to achieve, not the feature they click. Four people sit around every deployment.

Economic buyer

The CX or contact-center leader

"Cut my cost per contact and protect revenue without putting my brand at risk in front of customers." Measured on cost, CSAT, and not ending up in a headline. They will not go live until they trust the agent. Winning them is a trust problem before it is a technology problem.

Gatekeeper

IT and security

"Let this system into our stack without creating a security, data-residency, or compliance exposure." They block on SIP and static-IP requirements, CRM authentication, and knowledge-base sync. The silent killer of timelines. Their concerns have to surface in week one, not week ten.

End user

The customer who contacts support

"Resolve my issue now, and get me to a human the moment the agent cannot." The agent has to contain the routine and escalate the rest cleanly. Containment that frustrates customers is a false win.

Internal partner

The Forward Deployed Engineer

The FDE owns integrations and technical build. I own the strategy layer: which use cases, what success looks like, how to run the workshops, how to iterate after launch, what to feed back to the platform. One pod, two roles.

04Why this problem

RICE is a simple way to compare what to fund. Reach is how many it touches. Impact is how much it moves the goal. Confidence is how sure we are. Effort is the cost. Here is how I would read what a forward deployed PM could pour energy into at a new account, and why "get the first use case to production with proof" wins.

RICE read of forward deployed PM priorities
What I could prioritize Reach Impact Conf. Effort Read
Ship one high-volume, low-risk use case to production fast High High High Med Do this first. Highest score. Books real containment, proves the model to a skeptical buyer, unlocks expansion.
Scope a broad multi-use-case roadmap up front High High Low High Tempting and wrong. Low confidence until one case is live. Plan it, do not build it yet.
Build a bespoke custom capability for this one account Low Med Med High Only if it unblocks go-live. Otherwise it is the one-off trap that does not scale.
Deep optimization before any go-live Low Low Med High Premature. You cannot optimize an agent that is not in front of real traffic.

The winning move is the smallest deployment that proves real ROI and earns trust, because that is what converts a pilot into production and a production agent into expansion. Cresta's own tooling points the same way: Automation Discovery scores conversations by volume, complexity, and resolution rate and projects ROI, so the highest-value, lowest-risk first use case is identifiable from data, not opinion (Cresta, Dec 2025).

05Approach

I would not invent a new product. I would run Cresta's deployment lifecycle well and repeatably, using the assets Cresta already ships.

Within that lifecycle, three real bets shape each deployment. I would frame them as hypotheses and decide with data, not preference.

BET 1Scoping: pick the first use case from data, not the wish list

Customers arrive with either a vague mandate ("automate the contact center") or an over-narrow one ("just billing FAQs"). I would run Automation Discovery on real transcripts and choose the first use case on volume, resolution rate, and risk. Decide by the data: high volume, clean resolution path, low compliance exposure goes first.

BET 2Trust: treat the eval and test suite as the product surface

Containment is only a win if there are no safety failures. Cresta's Automated AI Agent Testing runs simulated visitors from de-identified historical calls, runs 15x more tests than human methods, and delivers 35% faster release cycles (Cresta, Sept 2025). I would define the test scenarios and eval bar with the customer before build, so go-live is a data conversation, not a leap of faith. This converts a 25%-trust buyer into a signed go-live.

BET 3Integration risk: surface IT and security blockers in week one

SIP and static IP, CRM authentication, API timeouts, and knowledge-base sync are the real timeline killers (Retell AI, 2026). I do not write the integration code; the FDE does. I run the stakeholder alignment that gets IT and security to "yes" early, and translate their constraints into requirements the FDE can build against. Map the stack against Cresta's deepest native integrations before kickoff, so we sequence around blockers instead of hitting them.

06What I'd ship first

For any new account, the smallest test of the biggest assumption is one production use case that proves real ROI under a real trust bar. The biggest assumption is always: this customer's traffic can be safely contained at a rate that pays for itself.

The first deployment, concretely

  • One high-volume, low-risk intent (for example order status or a billing FAQ), chosen from Automation Discovery data.
  • A defined containment target and an explicit safety bar agreed with the buyer before build.
  • An eval suite of simulated visitors the agent must pass before go-live.
  • Clean human escalation wired through the Agent Operations Center, which gives real-time visibility into AI and human conversations (Cresta, Dec 2025).
  • Hypercare in the first weeks, watching the live dashboards and tuning fast.
Why this first

It is the highest Reach times Impact times Confidence over Effort: a single, scoped, data-chosen use case is buildable in weeks, it produces a real containment number a CX leader can take to their board, and it earns the trust that unlocks the next use case. Containment typically starts modest at launch and climbs as the deployment matures and the agent is tuned. The first win is the foothold for that whole arc.

What it deliberately leaves out. No multi-use-case rollout, no bespoke custom capability unless it unblocks go-live, no deep optimization before live traffic exists. Those come after the first use case proves the model. Scoping them in early is the over-promise that kills first deployments.

07Metrics

The North Star is the metric that tells you the agent created value the customer will pay to keep. For a forward deployed PM that is net containment value per account: contacts safely resolved by the agent, valued in cost saved or revenue captured, net of any trust failures. It ties directly to renewal and expansion, which is what Cresta is funding this seat to drive.

Guardrails and anti-metrics. Containment is gamed if you push it at the cost of the customer. So I would hold three lines: CSAT of AI-handled conversations must not drop, the hallucination and safety-failure rate must stay below the bar agreed with the buyer, and the clean-escalation rate (handoffs that reach a human without the customer repeating themselves) must stay high.

08Business impact

These are hypotheses tied to the numbers in the brief. At roughly $100M ARR (Cresta, 2026), ARR growth is gated by deployment throughput and net revenue retention, not by lead volume. The forward deployed PM moves three of those levers directly.

  • Faster time-to-value pulls revenue forward and protects it. Every week saved on a deployment is captured containment value instead of stalled pilot. On the brief's contact economics, a 60-day acceleration on a large account is worth roughly $2M to $5M in annualized ROI to the customer, which is exactly the proof that drives renewal.
  • Post-launch optimization is the expansion engine. Containment typically starts modest at launch and climbs as the deployment matures and the agent is tuned. Cresta's Snap Finance customer reached a 5.5x improvement in containment, the kind of step-change that comes from iterative post-launch work, not the initial build (Cresta / Snap Finance).
  • Proven ROI is the renewal and reference story. Cox saw a 20% to 30% revenue-per-chat increase in residential sales and 10% to 20% for retention (Cresta / Cox). Achieve reached 3x ROI and a 75% reduction in after-call work (Cresta / Achieve). Outcomes like these are what a forward deployed PM is accountable for producing and documenting, and what defend Cresta against Sierra and Decagon at the renewal table.

09Repeatability

The strategic point of this role is that bespoke one-off services do not scale. Every deployment has to make the next one cheaper. That is the difference between a services cost center and a forward deployed team that compounds.

Cresta is already productizing this loop. Automation Discovery, Automated AI Agent Testing, and the Agent Operations Center are the company's attempt to turn the deployment playbook into product. A forward deployed PM is both a heavy user of those tools and a contributor who sharpens them with field learnings. I would treat "what reusable asset did this deployment leave behind" as a deliverable on every account, not an afterthought.

Listen and learn the machine

  • Shadow live deployments and read the existing playbooks, Automation Discovery outputs, and Agent Operations Center dashboards. Learn how Cresta actually deploys today.
  • Sit with FDEs to understand where builds and integrations get stuck, and which CCaaS and CRM integrations are deepest.
  • Map the metrics each account is judged on and where the current playbook is thin.
  • Get hands-on with the AI Agent build and testing tools so I can build and test, not just coordinate.
Days 30 to 60

A quick win and a sharper process

  • Take ownership of one live or near-launch account. Drive the first use case to go-live or to a measurable optimization step.
  • Run a scoping workshop end to end: pull conversation data, prioritize use cases, set the success criteria and safety bar with the customer.
  • Surface integration and security blockers early and translate them into requirements for the FDE.
Days 60 to 90

Ship, measure, and codify

  • Get a use case to production with a baseline containment and ROI number, and a clean escalation path.
  • Set the metric baseline and the guardrails for that account, and start the optimization loop.
  • Write up what was reusable: one playbook improvement and one piece of platform feedback. Make the next deployment faster.

11Risks

These are open assumptions about the work, and what I would test in week one.

Risk What I would validate first
Use-case fit. The first case might be lower-volume or higher-risk than the data suggests. Run Automation Discovery and read transcripts before committing to a target, and pressure-test the resolution path with the customer's own examples.
Trust and eval. The customer's safety bar might be stricter than the default eval suite. Agree the exact eval scenarios and the pass bar with the CX leader and compliance before build, so go-live is not a surprise.
Integration. A SIP, CRM-auth, or data-residency constraint could block go-live late. Run an IT and security review in the first two weeks and map the stack against Cresta's deepest native integrations.
Containment versus CSAT. Pushing containment could hurt customer satisfaction. Instrument CSAT and clean-escalation from day one and treat a CSAT drop as a stop condition.
Repeatability. A deployment might solve this account but leave nothing reusable. Define the playbook artifact and the platform-feedback item as deliverables at kickoff, not at the end.

12Questions

  3. Which CCaaS and CRM integrations are deepest and most production-proven today? That tells me where deployments are low-risk and where they need extra runway.
